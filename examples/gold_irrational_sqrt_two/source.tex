\section*{Irrationality of the square root of 2}

The square root of $2$ is irrational:
\[
\sqrt{2} \notin \mathbb{Q}.
\]

This is a classic public theorem --- item~1 on Freek Wiedijk's ``Formalizing 100 Theorems'' list
--- included here purely as a known-correct \emph{gold-reference} fixture for the audit harness. It
is \textbf{not} an original finance theorem and contains no new proof; the corresponding Lean
statement is a thin wrapper around an existing Mathlib theorem.

The statement asserts the \emph{irrationality} of $\sqrt{2}$ as a real number. It is not a rational
approximation such as $1.414 < \sqrt{2}$, and it is not the weaker fact $\sqrt{2} \neq 2$.
